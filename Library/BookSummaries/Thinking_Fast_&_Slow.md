1. Cognitive Dissonance: Incompatible Beliefs
- This is when we hold two beliefs that are incompatible with each other.
- If we don't get what we wanted, then we try to find excuse we anyways don't want it.
- Example of Didn't get the Job. Instead of accepting other person is better, try to find excuses like interview wasn't fair or we anyways never wanted this job.
- Diet Time. NO junk food. -- One Donut is OK.

2. Spotlight Effect: Everyone is watching me
- People overestimate how much others are observing them

3. Anchoring Effect: Secret Weapon of Sales & Negotiations
- Height of Redwood Trees
- Car dealers will start with higher price
- Set your mental anchor before buying a thing.

4. Halo Effect: Sequence of Information Matters
- Initial positive impressions of a person or thing, outweigh negatives.
- First impression lasts

5. Gambler's Fallacy: Is there a balance in the Universe?
- Believing future probabilities are affected by past events.
- Number of Heads in a row
- In a MCQ exam, continuous 5 times option C. Then you won't select option C for the 6th one.

6. The Contrast Effect: Comparisons Influence Judgements
- Judgments of something can be altered by the context it is presented in.
- Person will walk extra 10 minutes to save â‚¹100, but won't think twice if buying a suit of â‚¹9,900 or â‚¹10,000.
- It's easy to think something is attractive, large or expensive when it sits next to something ugly, small or cheap. Absolute judgments can be difficult to make.

7. Confirmation Bias: The Grand Poobah of all biases.
- Seeking and interpreting information that confirms existing beliefs
- Repeated things come closer to reality in our mind
- Political discourse
- Manual Cars vs Automatics
- To fix this:
	* Try "Thinking Gray".
	* Suspend Judgements, wait for more information before making judgements
	* Get your information from a variety of sources

8. Baader-Meinhoff Phenomenon: The Frequency Illusion
- Noticing something more after first learning about it
- Start noticing a car more after buying it

9. Zeigarnik Effect: Incomplete work vs the Mind
- Incomplete tasks stick in memory more than completed ones
	* Send email to client
	* Did I complete the assignment?
	* Did I lock the door?
- To cure this, make a plan. or set a reminder.

10. Paradox of Choice: Why less is more
- Too much analysis, leads to paralysis

11. Survivorship Bias: Mind Focus on the Winners
- Analyze both Loosers & Winners
- Ronaldo is amazing at free kicks... but do you know how many he has missed?

12. Self-Serving Bias: If Bad things happen, don't blame me.
- Our Failures are due to external forces, but our successes are the results of our internal actions.
- BJP didn't do good in 2024 General election, because of various factors by I.N.D.I Alliances, but 2019 was a huge win because of their bold actions.
- Got an 'A+' in result because of your hardwork, but got 'F-' because Teacher isn't good enough.
- For a CEO, when good result, it's because of his good policies. but when things goes south, The economy, interest rates & goverment policies are to blame.
- To combat this, the first step is to simply become aware of the bias. Try to practice humility and always seek accountabilitiy and feedback on your strengths and weeknesses from others.

13. Fundamental Attribution Error: It's not my fault
- We judge others on their character while attributing our failures and the failures of the ones we love on situational factors or on environment.
- If XYZ is late to work, it's because he's a lazy person and doesn't take his job seriously. But if you're late to work it's the traffic & weather's fault.

14. Hindsight Bias: Confidence creating memory distorter
- When we look at past events, everything seems clear and inevitable
- They are always experts after the event has occurred

15. Availability Bias: The lens you see the world through
- Humans are notoriously poor at thinking statistically, even when they are educated. We create a picture of the world using the examples that most esily come to mind. This is illogical, of course, and not in line with reality.
- We estimate the likelihood of something happening by the information most readily available in our memory.
	* Something in the News
	* Something negative
	* Something extreme
- Smoking isn't so bad, My uncle has smoked for 50 years and he is still alive.
- Chances of getting attack by Shark is 1 in 10,00,000. It's a stat. But if you see it on news, or someone in your close vicinity tells you about it.
- You'd overestimate the chances of a shark attack and underestimate the chances of dying from cancer.

16. Availability Cascade: A self-sustaining chain of events
- People begin to adopt the belief, not because it's true or prevalent, but because it's popular.
- Put more simply, an Availability Cascade is an enormous overreaction to a minor problem.
- News, Media are generally Availability Cascade.

17. Sunk Cost Fallacy: The escalation of commitment
- If you realized a movie is awful in first 25 minutes, but you keep watching it because you spent money on it. It's Sunk Cost Fallacy. You spent money either way, if you stay or go.
- Not giving up on a continually unfaithful partner. "I've invested so much time, energy and love. I can't give up now"
- The Sunk Cost Fallacy can especially plague investors decision making in the stock market.
- To be more rational, look at the current status and forecast of the stock or relationship. Don't put too much weight into the past investments of money, time energy or love.

18. The Framing Effect: It's all about presentation.
- We often draw different conclusions from the same information depending on how it's presented.
- Look out how News Outlets frame their headlines & stories. And how companies frame their offers.
- 99% FAT free OR 1% FAT ???
- It's NOT what you said, it's always the HOW you said it.

19. Clustering Illusion: Faces in the clouds
- Seeing Patterns that doesn't exist
- Ronaldo even looks like a goat.
- To overcome your sensitivity to pattern recognition, try to regain your skepticism. If you find a pattern, ask yourself if it is more likely to be pure chance or am I falling for the Clustering Illusion.
